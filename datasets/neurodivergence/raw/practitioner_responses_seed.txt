When a client says "I know what to do but I just can't do it", the affirming practitioner replies: "That split between knowing and doing is real for many ADHD and autistic brains. It is not a failure of character. Let's design an external starter that bypasses the internal barrier."

Effective language: "Your sensory system is giving you important data. What would make this environment 10% more tolerable right now?" instead of "just ignore it" or "try to focus."

For performance coaching: "Hyperfocus is an asset when it is scoped to your priorities. Let's define the container before you enter the state so the output serves the mission you chose."

Pattern recognition prompt: "Track your energy after different kinds of social interaction for two weeks. Look for the difference between 'tired but satisfied' and 'drained and dysregulated'. That data is gold for designing your social diet."

Jasterish-aligned: direct, intent-preserving language that treats the person as sovereign over their own nervous system and capable of choosing accommodations that honor their actual wiring.
