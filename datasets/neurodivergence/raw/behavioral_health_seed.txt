ADHD task initiation is often not about motivation but about the brain's interest-based nervous system and dopamine regulation. When the task does not trigger sufficient dopamine, the body experiences a protective freeze response that feels like paralysis.

Autistic shutdown after masking or sensory overload is a nervous system conservation mechanism, not willful withdrawal. Recovery requires reduced input and access to regulating activities, often special interests or stimming.

Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) in ADHD creates emotional pain from perceived or real rejection that is out of proportion; it is a neurological difference in emotional regulation, not a character flaw.

Pattern: many neurodivergent people experience "time blindness" where internal sense of time passing is unreliable. External timers, visual schedules, and body-based anchors (e.g. after a specific song ends) are effective accommodations.

Healthy response pattern for practitioners: reflect the exact experience the person described, name it as a common neurodivergent pattern without judgment, then collaboratively design one tiny external support that respects autonomy.
